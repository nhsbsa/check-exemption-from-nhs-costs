You could get help to pay for your NHS costs

Apply for further help

You can apply for help to pay for your NHS costs.

Your household income will be taken into account as well as your council tax and housing costs.

You don't have to be claiming benefits to apply.

Any help you get also covers your partner and children under 19.

You may also get help paying for:

  • NHS prescriptions
  • NHS dental treatment
  • sight tests
  • new glasses or contact lenses
  • repairing or replacing your glasses or contact lenses
  • NHS wigs and fabric supports
  • travel for NHS treatment

If your exemption or prescription prepayment certificate (PPC) is valid, you can use this service as proof.

You cannot use this service to:

  • renew your NHS exemption
  • renew your prescription prepayment certificate (PPC)
  • update your address or personal details - contact us to do this

Exemptions you can check

  • Prescription prepayment certificate (PPC)
  • Medical exemption certificate
  • Maternity exemption certificate
  • HC2 certificate (full help through the Low Income Scheme)
  • Tax Credit exemption certificate

Exemptions you cannot currently check

  • Prescription exemption certificate issued by the Ministry of Defence
  • HC3 certificate (partial help through the Low Income Scheme)

When you do not need to check

You're automatically entitled to free NHS prescriptions and NHS dental treatment if you are:

  • under 16
  • 16, 17 or 18 and in full-time education
  • getting certain benefits or you're named on the claim as a partner or child.
